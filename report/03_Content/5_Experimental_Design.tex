This chapter specifies the concrete configuration under which the
architecture of Chapter~\ref{ch:architecture} and the methodology of
Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology} are instantiated for the experiments
reported in Chapter~\ref{ch:results}: dataset preparation, domain
selection, hyperparameters, judge generation configuration, arena

Two headline research questions---arena ranking agreement (RQ1) and

uncertainty-driven scheduling recovers the same ranking at lower
judge-call cost) is \textbf{demoted} from the headline research
questions: the utility-based scheduler is an engineering mechanism
for judge-call economy, not a thesis-level contribution, and its
evaluation (C4, the random-scheduler ablation, and the

condition that a headline claim depends on.
Taxonomy-quality-vs-baselines, previously listed as RQ1, is
correspondingly demoted to DQ1, a construction-validity prerequisite
that must hold (the DAG must be geometrically coherent) for RQ1 and

C1--C3 (with C4 optional) specified in
Table~\ref{tab:arena-conditions} (\S\ref{sec:exp-arena-conditions}),
which isolate the contribution of geometric partitioning and
reference-informed judging from the contribution of the taxonomy
structure itself.

\section{Implementation Environment}\label{sec:exp-env}

The pipeline is implemented in Kotlin on Spring Boot, backed by
SQLite in WAL mode for the three persistent databases described in
Section~\ref{sec:arch-infra}.
Embeddings are produced by Qwen3-Embedding served locally via Ollama;
the judge model is Mistral-Large-3, accessed through an Azure OpenAI
endpoint.
All experiments are executed on [CPU/GPU spec, RAM, OS --- to be
finalised at submission].
Reproducibility follows the tuple convention of
Section~\ref{sec:arch-infra}: every run is seeded from
$\{42, 137, 2048\}$, and the canonical \texttt{TaxonomyConfig} values, selected via the
L9/Pareto tuning protocol of \S\ref{sec:exp-tuning-protocol}, are
\texttt{splitThreshold} $= 0.02$ (equivalently reported as 80 in
the pre-normalised internal scale), \texttt{minClusterSize} $= 25$,
\texttt{separationEpsilon} $= 0.08$, routing temperature $\tau = 2.0$,
\texttt{assignmentCosineGap} $= 0.03$ (equivalently
$\text{marginNats} \approx 1.5$), \texttt{tauFunnelFloor} $= 0.90$,
\texttt{refitMuPerIteration} enabled, and EMA
smoothing $\alpha_{\text{EMA}} = 0.5$ for maturity tracking.
Exact commit SHA and environment lockfile are recorded in
\texttt{REPRODUCIBILITY.md} and cited at submission.

\section{Datasets}\label{sec:exp-datasets}

\subsection{Primary Corpus: MMLU-Pro}

The primary corpus is MMLU-Pro~\parencite{wang2024mmlupro}, 12,000
questions across 14 domains with human-verified reference answers.
MMLU-Pro is used for two roles simultaneously: it supplies the
ground-truth domain labels that seed the 14 anchor nodes
(Section~\ref{sec:meth-migration}), and it supplies the per-domain
accuracy scores used as the external validation signal
(Section~\ref{sec:meth-validation}).
For the results reported in this thesis, taxonomy construction and
arena evaluation are run on the \textbf{Math} sub-corpus of MMLU-Pro:
1,079 queries after deduplication, chosen as the primary experimental
domain because it has among the largest per-domain query counts and
the clearest ground-truth answer format, minimising label noise in
the accuracy computation.

\subsection{Secondary Corpus: AG News}

AG News~\parencite{zhang2015agnews}, a four-class topic classification
corpus of news headlines, is used as a pipeline sanity check: it has
no MMLU-Pro-style domain structure and no reference-answer format
compatible with the reference-informed judge design, so it is not
used for anchor seeding or arena evaluation.
Its role is limited to verifying that the embedding, vMF fitting, and
splitting stages of the taxonomy pipeline execute correctly and
produce a geometrically coherent DAG on a corpus with known,
independently-labelled topic structure, as a check against
implementation bugs that MMLU-Pro's larger and noisier label space
might mask.

\section{Domain Selection and Split Protocol}\label{sec:exp-split}

\subsection{Domain Scope}

Taxonomy \textbf{construction} runs on the full 14-domain MMLU-Pro
corpus, not a single domain: all 14 anchors are seeded and refined
simultaneously, which is a precondition for the cross-domain
bridging and polyhierarchy machinery of
\S\ref{sec:meth-topology} to be exercised meaningfully, since
Source-B bridges are cross-anchor edges by definition and cannot
arise in a single-domain construction run.

The arena \textbf{evaluation} budget, by contrast, does not permit
judging all 14 domains at comparable statistical power. The arena
is run on 12 models restricted to \textbf{one to two ex-ante-selected,
high-support domain nodes}, chosen before observing any $\rho$ or
$\Delta\rho$ result under the rule of \S\ref{sec:exp-node-selection}.
This reflects a compute-budget trade-off: judging all 14 domains at
the canonical per-leaf budget multiplies judge-call cost by roughly
an order of magnitude for the same per-leaf statistical power.
The reported arena results are therefore a
\textbf{selected-domain proof-of-concept}, not a global validation
across all MMLU-Pro domains; this scope limitation is restated in
the Discussion.
Generalisation across all domains at matched arena power is
identified as future work in Section~\ref{sec:future-work}.

\subsection{Ex-Ante Node Selection Rule}\label{sec:exp-node-selection}

The one or two domain nodes evaluated in the arena are selected
before any $\rho$ or $\Delta\rho$ result is observed, using a fixed
rule so the choice cannot be construed as post-hoc cherry-picking.
A candidate node qualifies if it satisfies, in order: (i) high
held-out query support (sufficient arena-set queries to populate the
per-pair comparison budget at that leaf); (ii) high DAG
coherence/purity relative to sibling nodes; (iii) non-trivial
adapted-versus-canonical migration volume (a node with zero migrated
queries makes $\Delta\rho$ vacuous by construction); and (iv) model
performance variance on the node's queries under existing MMLU-Pro
per-model accuracy, so the ranking task is not degenerate.
Evaluating two qualifying nodes rather than one is preferred whenever
the judge-call budget allows, since a single node's $\Delta\rho$
value is a single proof-of-concept data point rather than a
distribution.

\subsection{Stratified Split}

The full 14-domain MMLU-Pro corpus is split 70/30 into a construction
set (used for taxonomy induction, migration scoring, and
reference-informed guideline generation) and an arena set (reserved
exclusively for held-out routing and pairwise evaluation); the
Math-domain counts (1,079 queries; 755/324 construction/arena) are
reported as an illustrative per-domain breakdown, since Math is one
of the two node candidates most likely to satisfy the selection rule
of \S\ref{sec:exp-node-selection}.
The split is stratified by the finest available sub-topic tag in the
MMLU-Pro metadata within each of the 14 domains, ensuring that all
partitions are proportionally representative before any geometric
restructuring occurs, and is fixed across all three random seeds to
isolate taxonomy-construction variance from split variance.

Taxonomy construction hyperparameters (\S\ref{sec:exp-hyperparams})
are selected using only construction-set-internal cross-validation;
no reserved arena-set label or accuracy signal is used at any stage
of hyperparameter selection, so that the L9/Pareto tuning protocol
of \S\ref{sec:exp-tuning-protocol} does not leak arena-set
information into the taxonomy that the arena later evaluates.
This severs the tuning-to-test leakage risk that would otherwise
undermine the answer-key-blind claim of
Table~\ref{tab:info-separation}: the reserved arena set is not
inspected by any Pareto gate, hard or soft.

The 30\% arena allocation is chosen so that the smallest leaf under
a selected node receives approximately 12 held-out queries.
At 12 evaluated models, this leaf presents $\binom{12}{2} = 66$
model pairs; with the connectivity bootstrap requiring at least one
comparison per pair and each of the 12 queries eligible for multiple
pairs, this establishes an upper bound of 792 judge calls
(66 pairs $\times$ 12 queries) at that leaf before any adaptive
scheduling logic is invoked, which is the binding constraint on
choosing a smaller arena fraction.
A sensitivity row repeats the canonical run at a 40\% arena split to
verify that headline Kendall $\tau$ and pairwise-winner results are
not an artefact of the specific split ratio.

\subsection{Hyperparameter Selection Protocol}\label{sec:exp-tuning-protocol}

The canonical values in Table~\ref{tab:hyperparams} are not
hand-picked; they are the output of an L9($3^4$) orthogonal-array
design over four factors---\texttt{assignmentCosineGap},
\texttt{minClusterSize}, \texttt{splitThreshold}, and
\texttt{tauFunnelFloor}---each tested at three levels, run against a
Pareto selection procedure (\texttt{tools/tuning/pareto.py}).
Hard gates reject configurations that violate structural invariants
(non-DAG output, anchor-topology violation, degenerate leaf count);
soft gates score surviving configurations on
\texttt{Top1Accuracy}, \texttt{RoutingECE}, and
\texttt{AvgMatchCount}, computed exclusively on
\textbf{construction-set-internal} cross-validation folds.
No reserved-arena-set label, accuracy, or judge verdict is used by
either the hard or soft gates at any point in the L9/Pareto
procedure; this is the mechanism by which
\S\ref{sec:exp-split}'s tuning/test-leakage boundary is enforced in
practice, not merely asserted.

\section{Reference-Informed Judge Generation Configuration}\label{sec:exp-judge}

The judge rubric generation procedure (Section~\ref{subsec:judge})
is instantiated as follows.
Representative-query packs are drawn from each leaf's construction
pool at pack size $k = 5$; a leaf with fewer than 5 construction
queries uses its full pool as a single pack.
The map-step LLM and the reduce-step LLM are the same underlying
model as the arena judge, Mistral-Large-3, to avoid introducing a
second model's stylistic biases into the rubric; this choice is
revisited as a limitation in Section~\ref{sec:arch-limits}.
The global structural template---covering presentation order, tie
handling, and output format---is fixed across all leaves and is
listed in full in Appendix~\ref{app:judge-template}.
Guideline generation is a one-time cost per frozen snapshot: rubrics
are computed once at snapshot-freeze time and reused for every
subsequent arena run against that snapshot, regardless of which
models are being compared.

\section{Arena Evaluation Configuration}\label{sec:exp-arena}

\subsection{Evaluated Models}

The canonical arena evaluates \textbf{12} models spanning open-weight
and proprietary families at comparable parameter-count tiers [full
model list and provider details in Appendix~\ref{app:models}], on
the one or two domain nodes selected under
\S\ref{sec:exp-node-selection}.
The full 14-domain construction run yields all leaves across all 14
anchors; only the leaves under the selected node(s) are included in
the arena's held-out routing target set.

\subsection{Scheduling and Budget}

The per-pair comparison budget is $B_{ij} = 20$ (\texttt{budget\_pair}
$= 20$), and the arena runs for a maximum of 10 scheduling rounds
(\texttt{maxRounds} $= 10$, matching the implementation default used
for this run) before the global escape valve is consulted.
Each logical comparison is realised as two judge calls under the
dual-call, position-reversed protocol of Section~\ref{subsec:judge}.
For a selected node with $\ell$ leaves, the theoretical judge-call
ceiling is $2 \times 20 \times \binom{12}{2} \times \ell = 2{,}640\ell$
calls if every pair at every leaf exhausted its full budget; for a
single 17-leaf node this is 44,880 calls, and for two selected nodes
the ceiling scales accordingly.
This ceiling is not the expected utilisation: the connectivity
bootstrap alone (one comparison per one of $\binom{12}{2} = 66$
pairs) already approaches or exceeds a leaf's available held-out
query pool at the 30\% split before utility-driven scheduling or
per-pair budget exhaustion becomes the binding constraint.
The ceiling figure is reported only as an upper bound for
provisioning API rate limits; the expected realised call count,
together with the per-leaf query-pool-to-pair-count ratio and its
effect on how many leaves reach \texttt{BUDGET\_EXHAUSTED} versus
\texttt{CONVERGED}, is reported directly in Chapter~\ref{ch:results}.

\subsection{Random Scheduler Ablation (Optional Diagnostic)}

Because the scheduler is not a headline contribution
(\S\ref{sec:exp-rqmap}), the random scheduler ablation (C4) is
reported only as an \textbf{optional secondary diagnostic}, not as a
condition any headline claim depends on.
When run, it replaces the composite-utility pair and query selection
of Section~\ref{sec:meth-scheduling} with uniformly random pair and
query selection, while retaining the same per-pair and per-leaf
budgets and the same three-level stopping policy, and is compared
against the adaptive scheduler at matched final judge-call counts on
total match count, achieved Kendall $\tau$/pairwise-winner accuracy
against MMLU-Pro, and rounds to convergence.
The budget-fidelity curve ($\rho$ or $\tau$ against cumulative judge
calls) and the Wilcoxon adaptive-vs-random significance test are
likewise demoted to optional secondary results.
An exhaustive round-robin condition (every pair against every routed
query, no stopping) is not included at all: it was already
computationally prohibitive under the original 16-model, 17-leaf
scope, and is strictly less justified now that the scheduler is not
a headline contribution.

\subsection{Verbosity Bias Probe}

Fifty judge matches are sampled uniformly from the canonical arena
run and re-judged with response position swapped, independent of the
standard dual-call protocol's own position randomisation, to produce
an out-of-protocol audit sample.
The position-flip rate (fraction of re-judged matches whose verdict
changes under the swap) and a per-model verbosity delta
$\Delta_{\text{verbosity}}$ (difference in win rate attributable to
response length after controlling for BT score) are reported.
This probe is specifically designed to confirm or quantify an anomaly
observed in preliminary runs (\texttt{arenabenchmark.log}) in which
one evaluated model's verdicts appeared to correlate with response
length independent of content quality; the probe is a targeted
diagnostic rather than a general-purpose bias correction.

second judge run, and it does not test a canonical-domain judge

\section{Baseline Conditions}\label{sec:exp-baselines}

Table~\ref{tab:baselines} lists the comparison conditions used to
contextualise TaxoArena's taxonomy quality (DQ1) against unsupervised
clustering approaches, all evaluated on the identical 70/30 split and
Qwen3 embeddings.
The originally planned supervised HTC baselines, HiAGM and HGCLR,
are \textbf{removed}: neither is implemented in the accompanying
codebase, and presenting unimplemented supervised baselines as
planned comparisons would overstate what this thesis delivers.
The remaining baselines---flat $k$-means, HAC (Ward), and the random
null---are reframed honestly as \textbf{system-level flat baselines}:
they do not share TaxoArena's trickle-routing pipeline, so they are
not equal-pipeline splitter baselines, only reference points for
whether TaxoArena's induced structure is non-trivially better than
simple unsupervised alternatives on the same embeddings.

against an ad-hoc gold structure.
DQ1 is instead led by reference-free metrics: Dendrogram Purity,
Weighted Leaf Purity, Spherical Silhouette, Total Dasgupta Cost,
Normalised Sackin Index, and Routing ECE
(\S\ref{sec:exp-metrics}), all of which score the taxonomy's internal
geometric coherence without requiring an external gold structure.
The flat $k$-means, HAC, and random baselines isolate the
contribution of TaxoArena's iterative vMF-based splitting and
topological optimisation relative to simpler unsupervised
alternatives on identical embeddings.

\section{Metrics and Statistical Protocol}\label{sec:exp-metrics}

\subsection{Taxonomy Quality Metrics}

Six reference-free metrics characterise the induced DAG independent
of arena evaluation, used for DQ1 in place of the gold-DAG-dependent
metrics (hierarchical F1, Edge F1, Triplet Accuracy) that were
originally planned but are not reportable absent a defined gold DAG
for MMLU-Pro (\S\ref{sec:exp-baselines}): Dendrogram Purity and
Weighted Leaf Purity; Spherical Silhouette; Total Dasgupta Cost;
Normalised Sackin Index (tree balance); and Routing Expected
Calibration Error (ECE) of the trickle-routing softmax against
ground-truth domain membership.

\subsection{Arena Quality Metrics}

Per-leaf and per-anchor BT scores are reported with their Fisher
information standard errors; Spearman $\rho$ against MMLU-Pro
accuracy is the headline validation statistic
(Section~\ref{sec:meth-validation}); $\Delta_{\text{verbosity}}$ and
judge Cohen's $\kappa$ (inter-call agreement under the dual-call
protocol) quantify judge reliability; total match count and matches
per leaf quantify budget efficiency.

\subsection{Aggregation and Significance Testing}

All headline numbers are reported as mean $\pm$ standard deviation
over the three seeds $\{42, 137, 2048\}$.
With 12 evaluated models, Spearman $\rho$ is weak and tie-sensitive
at this sample size, so Kendall $\tau$ is the preferred primary
rank-agreement statistic; pairwise-winner accuracy over
$\binom{12}{2} = 66$ pairs is reported as a descriptive (not
independent-sample) statistic, and top-$k$ Jaccard agreement for
$k \in \{3,5\}$ is reported alongside.
Spearman $\rho$ is retained only as a secondary statistic for
continuity with prior HTC literature.
If the optional random-scheduler diagnostic (C4) is reported, a
Wilcoxon signed-rank test on per-leaf Kendall $\tau$ compares the
adaptive scheduler against the random-scheduler ablation at matched
judge-call budgets; this non-parametric paired test is chosen over a
paired $t$-test because per-leaf rank-agreement values are bounded
and not assumed normally distributed across the small number of
leaves.
The previously pre-specified binary threshold of $\rho > 0.7$ is
\textbf{softened} to an interpretive target for strong agreement
rather than a pass/fail criterion: with only 12 models and one or
two selected nodes, $\rho > 0.7$ (or the corresponding $\tau$ range)
indicates strong agreement where achieved, but lower values are
still reported in full alongside Kendall $\tau$, pairwise-winner
accuracy, top-$k$ Jaccard, and bootstrap confidence intervals, and
any conclusion drawn is scoped to the selected domain node(s)
evaluated, not treated as a global validation of the arena's
discriminative power.
Bootstrap confidence intervals (2,000 resamples, percentile method)
are reported alongside every Kendall $\tau$, Spearman $\rho$, and
$\Delta\rho$ estimate to quantify sampling uncertainty given the
finite held-out query pool per leaf and the small model count.
Resampling is performed at the query level within domain: each
bootstrap resample draws held-out queries with replacement from
$Q_{D_k}^{\text{adapted}}$ (or $Q_{D_k}^{\text{canonical}}$ for Track
A), and all verdicts associated with a sampled query are carried into
the resample together, preserving the within-query dependence
structure noted as a methodological assumption in
Section~\ref{sec:meth-assumptions} rather than treating individual
verdicts as independent resampling units.

\section{Chapter Summary}

This chapter fixed, before data collection, the mapping from two
headline research questions (RQ1: arena ranking agreement; RQ2: the
migration effect $\Delta\rho$) plus one construction-validity
diagnostic (DQ1) to experimental condition and metric
(Table~\ref{tab:rq-map}), with the previously headline scheduling
question demoted to an optional secondary diagnostic; then specified
the concrete implementation environment; dataset preparation
(full 14-domain MMLU-Pro construction, AG News as a pipeline sanity
check); the reconciled scope---14-domain construction versus a
12-model arena on one or two ex-ante-selected domain nodes
(\S\ref{sec:exp-node-selection})---and 70/30 stratified split
protocol with its query-budget rationale and its construction-only
tuning boundary that prevents reserved-arena-set leakage into
hyperparameter selection; the canonical taxonomy construction
hyperparameters (as selected by the L9/Pareto tuning protocol,
\S\ref{sec:exp-tuning-protocol}) and ten-experiment ablation matrix,
including the reporting-sensitivity distinction for A3--A4 and the
new dag-max-mechanism ablations A9--A10; the reference-informed judge
generation configuration; the arena's model roster, scheduling
budget, the arena-level conditions C1--C3 (minimum defensible
package) plus optional C4 that isolate the contribution of geometric
partitioning and reference-informed judging
(Table~\ref{tab:arena-conditions}), the information-separation
protocol formalising the answer-key-blind design
(Table~\ref{tab:info-separation}), and the optional random-scheduler
and verbosity-bias diagnostic conditions; three taxonomy-structure
baseline comparisons restricted to unsupervised methods (HiAGM and
HGCLR removed as unimplemented) using reference-free DQ1 metrics in
place of the undefined-gold-DAG metrics; and the metrics and
statistical protocol---including Kendall $\tau$ and pairwise-winner
accuracy as the primary rank-agreement statistics at $M=12$, the
softened $\rho > 0.7$ interpretive target, the query-level bootstrap
resampling procedure, and the caveat on the theoretical judge-call
ceiling---used to interpret the results reported in
Chapter~\ref{ch:results}.
