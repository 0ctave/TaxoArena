reported in Chapter~\ref{ch:results}. Full taxonomy-tuning mechanics

Two headline research questions---arena ranking agreement (RQ1) and
the migration effect on that agreement (RQ2)---anchor the reported
results. A third question originally framed as RQ3 (adaptive
scheduling efficiency) is \textbf{demoted}: the scheduler is an
engineering mechanism for judge-call economy, not a thesis-level
contribution, and is reported only as an optional secondary
diagnostic where informative (Appendix~\ref{app:tuning-protocol}).
Taxonomy quality, previously listed as RQ1, is correspondingly
demoted to DQ1: a construction-validity prerequisite that must hold
for RQ1/RQ2 to be meaningful, but not itself a primary claim.
The scope of this thesis is a \textbf{selected-domain
proof-of-concept}: the arena evaluates one or two ex-ante-selected,
high-support domain nodes rather than all 14 MMLU-Pro domains at
matched statistical power, a compute-budget trade-off restated in
the Discussion and revisited in Section~\ref{sec:future-work}.

\section{Datasets and Split Protocol}\label{sec:exp-datasets}

The primary corpus is MMLU-Pro~\parencite{wang2024mmlupro}, 12,000
questions across 14 domains with human-verified reference answers,
used in two roles: it supplies the ground-truth domain labels that
seed the 14 anchor nodes, and it supplies the per-domain accuracy
scores used as the external validation signal
(Section~\ref{sec:meth-validation}). AG News~\parencite{zhang2015agnews}
serves only as a pipeline sanity check, verifying that embedding,
vMF fitting, and splitting execute correctly on an independently
labelled corpus; it is not used for anchor seeding or arena
evaluation.

Taxonomy \textbf{construction} runs on the full 14-domain corpus
simultaneously, a precondition for the cross-domain bridging
machinery of \S\ref{sec:meth-topology} to be exercised meaningfully.
The \textbf{arena} evaluates 12 models on one or two ex-ante-selected,
high-support domain nodes, chosen before observing any $\rho$ or
$\Delta\rho$ result under a fixed rule: (i) high held-out query
support; (ii) high DAG coherence relative to siblings; (iii)
non-trivial adapted-versus-canonical migration volume; (iv)
non-degenerate model performance variance under existing MMLU-Pro
accuracy. Math (1,079 queries after deduplication) is the primary
candidate, having among the largest per-domain query counts and
clearest ground-truth format.

The full corpus is partitioned into three disjoint, stratified
subsets: \textbf{construction} (60\%, taxonomy induction and rubric
generation), \textbf{tuning-validation} (15\%, L9/Pareto gates only),
and \textbf{arena-test} (25\%, held-out routing and judging only),
stratified by MMLU-Pro sub-topic tag and fixed across all three
seeds.\footnote{The 60/15/25 split is the intended design; the
runner's current default is \texttt{testRatio}$=0.3$ (70/30), and the
15\% tuning-validation partition is enforced by scoring configurations
on construction-internal metrics rather than as an explicit
materialised subsample. No arena-test query is seen by any tuning
gate under either realisation, so the no-leakage property holds;
setting \texttt{testRatio}$=0.25$ and materialising the explicit split
is a pending hardening step, not a correctness gap. Full detail in
Appendix~\ref{app:tuning-protocol}.} The arena-test fraction targets
approximately 10 held-out queries at the smallest leaf under a
selected node, the binding constraint given $\binom{12}{2}=66$ model
pairs per leaf. A sensitivity row repeats the canonical run at a
40\% arena-test split to check that headline results are not an
artefact of the split ratio.

\section{Arena Evaluation Configuration}\label{sec:exp-arena}

\subsection{Evaluated Models and Judge Generation}

The canonical arena evaluates \textbf{12} models spanning open-weight
and proprietary families at comparable parameter-count tiers (full
roster in Appendix~\ref{app:models}), on the domain node(s) selected
under \S\ref{sec:exp-datasets}. Judge rubrics are generated once per
frozen snapshot from representative-query packs ($k=5$ per leaf) using
the same Mistral-Large-3 model as the arena judge, and reused for
every subsequent arena run against that snapshot; the full structural
template is given in Appendix~\ref{app:judge-generation}.

\subsection{Scheduling and Budget}

The per-pair comparison budget is $B_{ij}=20$, over a maximum of 10
scheduling rounds before the global escape valve is consulted, with
each logical comparison realised as two dual-call, position-reversed
judge calls (Section~\ref{subsec:judge}). The theoretical judge-call
ceiling is $2{,}640\ell$ calls for $\ell$ leaves if every pair
exhausted its full budget; this is reported only as an upper bound
for API rate-limit provisioning, since the connectivity bootstrap
alone already approaches a leaf's held-out query pool at the 25\%
split before budget exhaustion becomes binding. Realised call counts
are reported in Chapter~\ref{ch:results}.

\subsection{Arena-Level Conditions (C1--C3)}\label{sec:exp-arena-conditions}

Table~\ref{tab:arena-conditions} specifies the minimum defensible
condition set isolating, one factor at a time, the contribution of
geometric re-partitioning (C2) and reference-informed judging (C3) to
the RQ1/RQ2 result. All conditions share the identical frozen
snapshot, held-out query pool, and model roster; only the listed
factor varies. The random-scheduler ablation and the verbosity-bias
probe are optional secondary diagnostics, not part of this minimum
package, and are reported in Appendix~\ref{app:tuning-protocol} where
run.

of domain-conditioned, reference-informed judging by holding the
taxonomy and grouping fixed and varying only the judge prompt,
addressing the concern that RQ1's result could be confounded with
generic-judge quality.

\section{Baseline Conditions}\label{sec:exp-baselines}

Table~\ref{tab:baselines} lists the comparison conditions used to
contextualise taxonomy quality (DQ1) against unsupervised clustering,
all evaluated on the identical split and embeddings. The originally
planned supervised HTC baselines, HiAGM and HGCLR, are removed as
unimplemented in the accompanying codebase. The remaining
baselines---flat $k$-means, HAC (Ward), and a random null---are
system-level flat baselines: reference points for whether TaxoArena's
induced structure improves non-trivially on simple unsupervised
alternatives, not equal-pipeline comparisons.

is led instead by the reference-free metrics of
Section~\ref{sec:exp-metrics}.

\section{Metrics and Statistical Protocol}\label{sec:exp-metrics}

\textbf{Taxonomy quality (DQ1):} Dendrogram Purity, Weighted Leaf
Purity, Spherical Silhouette, Total Dasgupta Cost, Normalised Sackin
Index, and Routing ECE.

\textbf{Arena quality (RQ1/RQ2):} per-leaf and per-anchor BT scores
with Fisher information standard errors; Spearman $\rho$ and Kendall
$\tau$ against MMLU-Pro accuracy; $\Delta_{\text{verbosity}}$ and
judge Cohen's $\kappa$ for judge reliability; total match count and
matches per leaf for budget efficiency.

\textbf{Aggregation and significance:} all headline numbers are
reported as mean $\pm$ standard deviation over the three seeds. With
12 evaluated models, Kendall $\tau$ is the preferred primary
rank-agreement statistic over Spearman $\rho$ (weak and tie-sensitive
at this sample size); pairwise-winner accuracy over $\binom{12}{2}=66$
pairs and top-$k$ Jaccard ($k\in\{3,5\}$) are reported alongside;
Spearman $\rho$ is retained only for continuity with prior HTC
literature. Bootstrap confidence intervals (2,000 resamples,
percentile method) accompany every Kendall $\tau$, Spearman $\rho$,
and $\Delta\rho$ estimate, resampled at the query level within domain
to preserve within-query verdict dependence. The previously
pre-specified $\rho > 0.7$ threshold is softened to an interpretive
target for strong agreement, not a pass/fail criterion; results below
it are still reported in full and scoped to the selected domain
node(s), not treated as a global validation claim.

\section{Chapter Summary}\label{sec:exp-summary}

This chapter fixed the mapping from two headline research questions
(RQ1, RQ2) and one construction-validity diagnostic (DQ1) to
experimental condition and metric, then specified the implementation
environment, dataset and split protocol, the arena's model roster,
scheduling budget, and minimum defensible condition set (C1--C3), the
information-separation protocol formalising the answer-key-blind
